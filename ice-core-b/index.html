The thickness of the annual layers in ice cores can be used to derive a precipitation rate, which is essential parameter for many past climate studies.

Scientists run melted samples through various instruments to find tiny pieces of pollution, like sulfates, traces of metals, or natural aerosols like dust or volcanic ash . After analyzing enough ice core slices, a researcher can look for patterns to track changes in the atmosphere's composition and temperature.
